RailTrack — FYP Deployment Platform
Final Year Project · 2024 – Present

Description: A self-hosted web platform to manage Final Year Projects at university level. Handles container deployment via Docker, GitHub/GitLab submissions, milestone tracking, feedback loops, and three-tier role access.

What I Learned: Docker API integration via ProcessBuilder, Server-Sent Events (SSE) for real-time updates, BCrypt password hashing, and building a complete SDLC for a real-world system.

Tools Used: Java EEJSPMySQLDockerTomcatBootstrap 5JDBC

Job Analysis Portfolio — EOP Course
EOP Coursework · 2025

Description: Analysed and compared 4 real job postings from Malaysian tech companies — Intel Graduate Talent, Pentamaster Automation Software Engineer, Toray Software Engineer, and Eonmetall Programmer Engineer.

What I Learned: How to extract requirements from job descriptions, identify skills gaps, and write professional justifications for job fit assessments.

Tools Used: MS WordJob Analysis TemplateSkills Matching

What Improved Over Time

My ability to communicate professionally in writing has grown significantly. Early drafts of my resume and cover letters were too informal — over the weeks I learned how to write concisely, use action verbs, and quantify achievements. The job analysis exercises also forced me to think critically about how my skills align with industry needs.

Lessons Learned

One of the biggest lessons from this course is that technical skills alone aren't enough. Being able to articulate what I do, why it matters, and how it benefits an employer is just as important as writing the code. EOP has given me tools to bridge that gap — especially through resume writing, job analyses, and self-introduction exercises.

Personal Thoughts

Going through this course while finishing my Final Year Project was challenging but valuable. RailTrack taught me how to think about systems at scale; EOP taught me how to present that thinking to the world. Both skills will matter when I step into the industry. I feel more prepared now than I did at the start of the semester.
